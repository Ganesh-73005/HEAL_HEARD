Why is it necessary to detect disease in cattle ?

Detecting disease in cattle is essential to ensure their health and well-being, preventing suffering and reducing mortality rates. It also impacts economic productivity by maintaining milk production and growth. Early detection safeguards food safety by ensuring healthy meat and dairy products. Additionally, it helps control disease spread, protecting entire herds from outbreaks. Overall, this practice supports sustainable farming and responsible antibiotic use.
